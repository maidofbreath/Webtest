Good Cards, Best Friends

My Favorite Cat Spells

Magic cards have quite a few words on them. This makes sense, if it was just pictures or hyroglyphs we wouldn't be able to do almost anything that makes magic unique. Some of these words serve to sort cards into categories, words that I will refer to as "types" from now on. While there are a few uncommon card types, the card types you will certainly see in any game will be the following:

  • Instant
  • Sorcery
  • Creature
  • Artifact
  • Enchantment

The type that we will be focusing on will be creature more specifically the subtype "Cat".

Why Cats?

To get it out of the way, I still love pirates. I will always love pirates for god's sake they're pirates! Pirates come with many of the best grixis elements (thats red blue black) and they will always have a steady place in my heart, but cats... Cats are all encompassing. Cats are not limited by your silly color wheels, cats roam where they wish. If a cat wants to wizard, a cat will wizard. If a cat wants to warrior, there's a whole clan of cats that want to warrior. Cats can do what ever. They will do whatever. And they will do it well. So, I proclaim with a bias that can only be held by a child that was steadfast in her loyalty to the Riverclan, Cats are the best creature type in magic. With that, I will list the cats that I have the most fun with.

Rin and Seri, Inseperable

I'm going to start the body of this raving wall of text by stating my bias is in commander. While there are plenty of formats I've played and many more I have not, commander is the format I play the most. That being said, the other thing I need to get out of the way is that this is a dog. And a cat! But a dog nonetheless. This additional subtype just proves my point about the versatility of cats in Magic, if cats want to dog, they can dog! My love for this card comes first from the card art and story. While not thought out charaters in the magic mythos, Rin and Seri were abandoned by their owners and ended up finding eat other in the wild. After gaining each other's trust, they became a team. Wonderful. While I cannot deny there are cooler characters in the Magic: The Gathering universe (We will get to a cool one later), I greatly appreciate these goobers. In terms of ability, the card itself insists that each cat you cast must be accompanied by a dog and vice versa. It creates a little token of the accompanying creature, with a token being a nebulous card with dedicated abilities. Tokens are a very big part of naya decks (red green white) and admittedly my favorite mechanic to play with. While the ability is pretty good on its own, cards like Anointed Procession or Pack Leader make this deck run wild and free. I cannot emphasize how many cards make this deck run like its leg braces just fell off. The only downside is how fragile creatures are in Magic. Rightfully so, but there's not much 48 dog tokens can do against a Blasphemous Act.

Ocelot Pride

This card is word soup. I don't like the concept of word soup cards. However, the utility of this feline is undeniable. The first thing worth mentioning is the cost. It is one white. That's one of the easiest resources to acquire in any deck with white, as any deck with white will have more white than any other color. You know what is not one white mana? Every other token doubler. Seriously, all of them. While Ocelot pride comes with a lot of restrictions before you can access this token doubling (having 10 or more permanents, gaining life, the cat not dying) all of these criteria are easy to meet in a white token deck. Seriously. White, among a few other things, is the life gain color, and inherently you play a token doubler in a token deck, where cheesing out a lot of cheap permanents is the name of the game. Not only is it a cheap token doubler (not literally this thing can go for over 100$ if you want it pretty) but it makes a token on its own. It is a tremendous amount of value in one card. If cats want to value, they will value. In all seriousness this card is incredibly self sustaining and can become unbelievable with just a little help.

Ygra, Eater of All

Firstly, this is going to be the only occurence of a cat with black in its color identity on its card. While ideally I want to showcase all of the colors of cats to really reinforce the cats doing whatever they want idea, I am going to end up leaving blue out of this list. I simply don't have a lot of experience with any of the blue cats and cats primarily tend to hang out in the white color identity (they like the warmth of the sun you see). Rest assured, if cats want to editor in chief, they will editor in chief. With that out of the way, lets talk about Ygra. Recall during my endorsement of Rocco, I mentioned the amount of shenanigans you can get into with food tokens. Dude, they're all food. This particular brand of all food shenanigans offers a LOT more than it appears to. Ignoring the value on the card itself with the buff ability, it gives you an easy sarifice outlet to utilize cards like Fleshtaker. More importantly, it increases your ability to interact with other people's boards. It's good practice to run artifact and creature removal, now these categories overlap! Once I played a game where Rin and Seri was on my board and Ygra on an opponents. While Ygra was out, I played Qasali Slingers and proceeded to wipe out my competition using someone elses card. I imagine this card does alright in an Ygra deck anyways. So if you were wondering, if a cat wants to greedy, cat will greedy.

Ajani, Nacatl Pariah

I'm going to be honest here, this is just the goat of cats. If cats want to-
The card I chose is just because it is technically the only Ajani card that is a cat. To be precise, it is the only Ajani card with the subtype cat because most Ajani cards have the subtype Ajani. He is that guy. Ajani is what is known as a planeswalker. Mechanic wise, planeswalkers are distinct from creatures due to their ability to be attacked directly. Technically, the player is a planeswalker, but mechanically you cannot use Dreadbore to make your opponent lose the game. Planeswalkers also cannot attack or block, and if swung at must have blockers declared or lose loyalty counters. The controller of a planeswalker can manipulate loyalty counters once per turn to activate abilities on the card, either increasing the counter or using the counters to pay a cost. When loyalty counters reach zero, the planeswalker goes to the graveyard. Most notable planeswalker cards have nice benefits to +counter abilities and have crazy, high cost, game changing -counter abilities. While Ajani has great staple white abilities, his power lies in his story. Planeswalkers get their name from their ability to...walk planes. Planes (not plains) are essentially different dimensions that encapsulate the subject of magic sets. One may get the ability to travel through planes by having some crazy powerful awakening called igniting. Ajani ignited when he was betrayed by his clan and blamed for the death of his brother, the sorrow of losing everything he loved transforming him. He uses his power to unite and heal communities in a vast array of planes. He's a chill guy.
